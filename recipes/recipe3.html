Kwasnica soup

Description

Kwasnica is a traditional Polish highlanders soup made with sauerkraut and pork ribs, perfect for warming up during cold winter days. This hearty soup is rich in smoky, sour flavors, making it a beloved comfort food in Poland.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound raw pork ribs
  • 1/2 pound smoked pork ribs
  • 1/4 pound smoked bacon, diced
  • 4 cups sauerkraut, roughly chopped
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup dried forest mushrooms, soaked in warm water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 5 allspice berries
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
  • 1 teaspoon dried marjoram
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Preparation steps

    Prepare the Broth:

  1. In a large pot, combine the raw pork ribs, smoked pork ribs, smoked bacon, and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes, skimming any foam that rises to the surface.

    2. Add Aromatics:

  2. Add the onion, garlic, bay leaves, allspice, and caraway seeds to the pot. Simmer for another 30 minutes.

    3. Add Sauerkraut and Mushrooms:

  3. Stir in the sauerkraut and soaked mushrooms (with soaking water). Continue to simmer for 20 minutes.

    4. Add Potatoes:

  4. Add the diced potatoes to the pot and cook for an additional 20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

    5. Season:

  5. Stir in the dried marjoram, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

    6. Serve:

  6. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot with hearty rye bread.
